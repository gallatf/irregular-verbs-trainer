How verbs are selected

The app uses spaced repetition (SM-2 algorithm): each verb you answer correctly is scheduled to reappear after a growing interval — 1 day, then 4 days, then roughly doubling each time. A miss resets the interval to 1 day. This ensures you review each verb just before you'd forget it, which is the most efficient way to build long-term memory.

Within a session, harder verbs (recent misses) are picked more often than easy ones. The same verb never appears twice in a row.

Near-miss retry (Type it mode)

If your answer is only one character wrong, or has two adjacent characters swapped (e.g. borken instead of broken), you get a second attempt. The amber border shows which field needs fixing. Getting it right on the retry counts as a correct answer.

Progress & persistence

Progress is saved automatically in your browser after every card. It is always available offline — no account needed.

To sync across devices, use the Log in button at the top of the page. After signing in, progress is also saved to the server after every card and restored automatically when you open the app on any device.
